# Writing About Your Project

Writing about your open source work helps others discover it, understand it, and decide whether it's useful for them. It also builds Nava's presence in the civic tech and open source communities.

## Blog Posts and Articles

### When to Write
- At project launch — introduce the problem and your approach
- At major milestones — new features, architecture changes, lessons learned
- When you solve an interesting technical challenge
- When you learn something that could help others

### Where to Publish
- **Nava's blog**: For posts about Nava's approach and impact
- **Technical blogs** (dev.to, Medium, personal blogs): For technical deep dives
- **Government technology outlets**: For posts about civic tech and government modernization

### What Makes a Good Post
- Start with the problem, not the solution
- Explain who benefits and how
- Include concrete examples or code snippets
- Be honest about trade-offs and limitations
- Link to the repository so readers can explore further

## Conference Talks and Meetups

Presenting at conferences and meetups is a powerful way to share your work:

- **Civic tech meetups**: Code for America Brigade events, local civic tech groups
- **Government technology conferences**: Focus on impact and lessons learned
- **Open source conferences**: Highlight the technical approach and community aspects
- **Internal Nava talks**: Share learnings with your colleagues first as practice

## Case Studies

Government technology projects have compelling stories to tell:

- What was the problem the agency faced?
- How did the open source approach make a difference?
- What impact did the project have on the people it serves?
- What can other agencies or organizations learn from the experience?

Case studies require sensitivity — coordinate with your government agency partner before publishing.
