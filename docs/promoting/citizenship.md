# Open Source Citizenship

Being part of the open source community is about more than publishing code. Good citizenship means contributing to the health of the ecosystem that we all depend on, supporting the projects and people around us, and bringing Nava's commitment to public benefit into everything we do in the open.

## Being a Good Community Member

- **Be respectful**: Treat everyone with courtesy, whether they're beginners or experts
- **Be constructive**: When providing feedback, focus on the work, not the person
- **Be patient**: Not everyone has the same context or experience level you do
- **Be generous**: Share knowledge freely and help others learn

## GitHub Profile and Presence

Nava's GitHub presence is part of our public identity:

- Keep the [navapbc organization](https://github.com/navapbc) profile up to date
- Pin important repositories so visitors find them easily
- Use clear, descriptive repository names and descriptions
- Archive (don't delete) projects that are no longer maintained

## Engaging with the Civic Tech Community

We're part of a broader ecosystem of organizations working together to improve government technology. These are our partners and peers:

- **Code for America**: Engage with Brigade events and community projects
- **18F and USDS**: Follow and support other government digital service teams' open source work
- **Government open source**: Participate in initiatives like code.gov and federal open source policies
- **Academic partnerships**: Collaborate with researchers studying civic technology

## Nava's Open Source Identity

When engaging publicly on behalf of Nava:

- Represent Nava's values: transparency, public benefit, quality, and inclusion
- Don't disparage competitors or other organizations
- Be thoughtful about what you endorse — your GitHub stars and follows are public
- Remember that your contributions and comments are associated with Nava
