# Contribution Recordkeeping

Tracking your open source contributions helps Nava measure impact, maintain compliance awareness, and recognize the work our team members do in the broader community.

## Why Track Contributions

- **Impact measurement**: Understanding where and how Nava contributes to the open source ecosystem
- **Contract awareness**: Some government contracts have specific requirements around IP and code sharing
- **Recognition**: Contributions represent real work that benefits Nava and the community
- **Compliance**: Knowing what CLAs have been signed and what projects Nava contributes to

## What to Record

For each significant contribution, note:

- **Project**: Name and repository URL
- **Date**: When the contribution was made
- **Type**: Code, documentation, bug report, review, etc.
- **Description**: Brief summary of what was contributed
- **CLA signed**: Whether a CLA was required and signed
- **Related to Nava work**: Whether the contribution relates to a Nava project or contract

Minor contributions (e.g., fixing a typo in docs) don't need formal tracking.

## How to Report

Follow the process established by your team lead for reporting contributions. At minimum, contributions should be visible through your GitHub activity. For contributions that may have contract implications, discuss with your engineering lead.
